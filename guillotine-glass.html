How It Works

Vertical Opening Mechanism

This motorized window features a vertical opening mechanism, with panels that slide upward from the bottom. Their movement is facilitated by tracks installed on the side frames.

While guillotine-style windows are a novel product in Southern Europe, they have long been popular in the United States and Northern Europe. Nevertheless, their use is becoming increasingly widespread in countries like Spain.

At Costa Glass, we have completed numerous quality projects that attest to our expertise in this field of closures.

KG-70 Model

Blending Tradition and Modernity

The KG-70 window is a prime example of guillotine-style design, composed of two panels (or "leaves") that slide vertically when opened. Historically, these windows relied on weights and pulleys for effortless operation. Today, this system has evolved, replacing weights with adjustable counterbalances tailored to the window's weight.

When opened, the panels slightly overlap, ensuring an airtight seal while allowing efficient airflow throughout the building. Costa Glass's KG-70 window seamlessly blends tradition and modernity, offering a functional and aesthetically pleasing design to elevate any space.
